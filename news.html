Forest Conservation April 2, 2026

Milestone Reached: 100,000 Indigenous Trees Planted in Mwanza.

Thanks to the incredible efforts of our local volunteers, corporate partners, and the Mwanza City Council, Mr Tree Foundation has officially surpassed our goal for the first quarter.

Thanks to the incredible efforts of our local volunteers, corporate partners, and the Mwanza City Council, Mr Tree Foundation has officially surpassed our goal for the first quarter. This massive planting drive focused on restoring degraded hillsides and securing vital water catchments around Lake Victoria.

Over 5,000 community members participated in the week-long event, demonstrating the power of grassroots environmental action. The trees planted include drought-resistant indigenous species carefully selected by local botanists to ensure maximum survival rates.

We are actively monitoring the newly planted zones to ensure proper root establishment. This milestone proves that when the community comes together, we can genuinely reverse the effects of deforestation.

Clean Energy

March 28, 2026

Distributing 500 Clean Cookstoves to Rural Households.

Our latest initiative successfully replaced hazardous charcoal stoves with clean, ethanol-based alternatives, directly improving the health of women and reducing pressure on local forests.

Our latest initiative successfully replaced hazardous charcoal stoves with clean, ethanol-based alternatives, directly improving the health of women and reducing pressure on local forests.

Indoor air pollution from cooking with solid fuels is a major health crisis in rural Tanzania. By providing subsidized clean cookstoves, we are protecting families from respiratory illnesses while simultaneously drastically cutting down the demand for charcoal.

This project was made possible by our generous partners in the green energy sector. Over the next year, we aim to distribute an additional 2,000 units across the wider Lake Zone region.

Waste Management

March 15, 2026

Community Youth Turn Plastic Waste into Paving Bricks.

See how a group of young innovators in Mwanza are utilizing our circular economy grants to melt down collected lake plastic and mold it into durable construction materials.

See how a group of young innovators in Mwanza are utilizing our circular economy grants to melt down collected lake plastic and mold it into durable construction materials.

Rather than seeing plastic bottles as trash, these entrepreneurs view them as raw materials. By mixing melted plastic with sand, they create paving bricks that are stronger and more affordable than standard concrete.

The foundation is proudly supporting this initiative by providing collection gear and funding for specialized melting equipment. This is a perfect example of green livelihoods solving environmental issues.

Water Protection

March 04, 2026

The Great Coastal Cleanup: 5 Tons of Debris Removed.

Over 200 volunteers gathered last weekend to clear illegal dumping sites along the shores of Lake Victoria, restoring critical wetland habitats and protecting local fisheries.

Over 200 volunteers gathered last weekend to clear illegal dumping sites along the shores of Lake Victoria, restoring critical wetland habitats and protecting local fisheries.

The cleanup targeted 4 major coastal sectors that were heavily choked by domestic and industrial runoff. Volunteers worked tirelessly from dawn until dusk, segregating recyclable plastics from general waste to ensure proper disposal.

We extend our deepest gratitude to the Mwanza City Council for providing transport trucks, and to every single volunteer who dedicated their Saturday to protecting our lake.

Green Livelihoods

February 22, 2026

Sustainable Beekeeping Program Funds Local Schools.

By placing apiaries near protected forest zones, rural women have generated a sustainable income from honey sales, proving that conservation pays.

By placing apiaries near protected forest zones, rural women have generated a sustainable income from honey sales, proving that conservation pays.

This initiative serves two vital purposes: the bees act as crucial pollinators for the regenerating forests, and the honey provides a premium product that is sold in local markets. The profits have recently been used to purchase school supplies for over 50 children in the village.

We plan to scale this model to three new villages before the end of the year, providing training and starter hives to women's cooperatives.

Climate Change

February 10, 2026

Farmers Receive Training on Drought-Resistant Crops.

To combat changing weather patterns, Mr Tree Foundation hosted a 3-day agricultural summit teaching climate-smart farming techniques to over 300 local farmers.

To combat changing weather patterns, Mr Tree Foundation hosted a 3-day agricultural summit teaching climate-smart farming techniques to over 300 local farmers.

With rainfall becoming increasingly unpredictable, traditional crops are failing. Our agronomists introduced specialized drought-resistant seeds and taught advanced water retention techniques like deep mulching and terracing.

The transition to climate-smart agriculture ensures that food security is maintained even in the face of harsh, changing environmental conditions.

We are thrilled to announce a formal partnership that will scale our water protection programs across borders over the next 5 years.

Mwanza Shores
