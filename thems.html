Our Pillars of Impact

Our Themes

Our Strategy

Everything in nature is connected.

You cannot solve deforestation without addressing energy needs. You cannot protect Lake Victoria without managing urban waste.

Our six themes are not separate projects—they are deeply interwoven pillars designed to transform Tanzania's ecosystems and economies simultaneously.

Holistic Vision

Tackling the root causes of environmental degradation across the Lake Zone, rather than just treating the symptoms.

Local Action

Applying global scientific principles directly to the local communities and farmers living in Mwanza.

01

Forests, Trees &
Biodiversity Conservation

Tanzania is home to incredibly diverse ecosystems that are currently under threat from rapid deforestation. We are committed to restoring depleted woodlands, protecting existing flora and fauna, and massively expanding urban green spaces.

  • Large-scale community tree planting
  • Protection of indigenous tree species
  • Wildlife habitat restoration projects
02

Climate Change
Mitigation & Adaptation

Changing weather patterns severely impact agricultural communities across the Lake Zone. We work to mitigate greenhouse gas emissions while simultaneously equipping farmers and communities with the tools needed to adapt to a changing climate.

  • Climate-smart agriculture training
  • Carbon offsetting initiatives
  • Extreme weather preparedness
03

Waste Management &
Circular Economy

Pollution is a visible threat to our cities and natural landscapes. We view waste not as trash, but as a resource. By promoting a circular economy, we help communities turn discarded materials into wealth while cleaning up our environment.

  • Plastic recycling initiatives
  • Community clean-up drives
  • Upcycling workshops for youth
04

Water Resources
Protection & Management

Lake Victoria is the lifeblood of Mwanza. Protecting this vital resource from industrial and domestic pollution is paramount. We advocate for clean water access and educate communities on watershed protection.

  • Lake shore anti-pollution campaigns
  • Safe drinking water initiatives
  • Wetland conservation
05

Clean &
Renewable Energy

Reliance on charcoal and firewood is a primary driver of deforestation in Tanzania. We advocate for and facilitate the transition to clean, accessible, and affordable renewable energy sources for rural and urban households.

  • Promotion of solar technologies
  • Biogas system education
  • Fuel-efficient cookstove distribution
06

Green Livelihoods &
Green Economy

Environmental conservation cannot succeed if it ignores economic reality. We empower women and youth by creating sustainable businesses and green jobs that allow them to earn a living while protecting nature.

  • Eco-entrepreneurship training
  • Sustainable beekeeping and agroforestry
  • Support for eco-tourism initiatives
